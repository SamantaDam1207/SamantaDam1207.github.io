About Me

I am an Electrical Engineering student at the University of Toronto, with a passion for designing and building interactive robots. My technical skills include programming in C++, Verilog, Arduino C++, and more. I have worked on numerous projects, from robot construction to software-defined radios and plant monitoring systems.

Projects

5 ft Interactive Robot

Designed and built a 5 ft tall robot with omnidirectional wheels and robotic arms. Programmed with Arduino C++ and controlled via a custom MIT App Inventor interface.

Software-Defined Radio (SDR)

Collaborated with several teams to create a Software-Defined Radio, working on the Local Oscillator, Microcontroller, and User Interface. Designed the PCB with Altium and Multisim.

Game of Hearts (C++)

Developed a Hearts card game with 3 AI bots and 1 user-controlled player. Features menus for pausing, difficulty selection, and restarting, using C++ and VGA output.

Plant Monitoring System

Led the development of a system to monitor plant conditions with sensors (Soil Moisture, Temperature, Humidity) and controlled it via a Bluetooth app. Built a custom frame.

Taylor Series Expansion Calculator

Developed a Finite State Machine (FSM) and datapath using Verilog for a calculator that displays a Taylor Series expansion with graphing features on a VGA output.

Park Storage Solution

Collaborated with the City of Toronto Parks Forestry and Recreation department to design a park storage solution. Delivered a final product that improved park logistics.

Building Accessibility Redesign

Led a team to propose 3 entrance redesigns to improve building accessibility. Created project documentation and delivered a final proposal for future development.
